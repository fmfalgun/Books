## PART 1: TIME & DISTANCE + TRAINS/BOATS (5 Questions)

### Type 1: Simple Speed-Distance-Time Problems

**Q1.** A car travels at 60 km/h for 2.5 hours. How far does it travel?
A) 120 km
B) 150 km
C) 180 km
D) 200 km

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Distance = Speed × Time = 60 × 2.5 = 150 km

**Q2.** A runner covers 400 meters in 50 seconds. What is his speed in km/h?
A) 28.8 km/h
B) 32.4 km/h
C) 36 km/h
D) 40 km/h

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** Speed = 400/50 = 8 m/s = 8 × 3.6 = 28.8 km/h

### Type 2: Relative Speed (Trains)

**Q3.** Two trains are moving towards each other at speeds of 60 km/h and 40 km/h respectively. They are 150 km apart. In how much time will they meet?
A) 1.2 hours
B) 1.5 hours
C) 2 hours
D) 2.5 hours

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Relative speed = 60 + 40 = 100 km/h; Time = 150/100 = 1.5 hours

**Q4.** A train 150 meters long is moving at 72 km/h. How long will it take to cross a 250-meter platform?
A) 18 seconds
B) 20 seconds
C) 25 seconds
D) 30 seconds

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Total distance = 150 + 250 = 400 m; Speed = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s; Time = 400/20 = 20 seconds

### Type 3: Boats & Streams

**Q5.** A boat travels upstream at 6 km/h and downstream at 14 km/h. What is the speed of the boat in still water and the speed of the stream?
A) Boat: 10 km/h, Stream: 4 km/h
B) Boat: 8 km/h, Stream: 6 km/h
C) Boat: 12 km/h, Stream: 2 km/h
D) Boat: 9 km/h, Stream: 5 km/h

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** Boat speed = (6 + 14)/2 = 10 km/h; Stream speed = (14 - 6)/2 = 4 km/h

## PART 2: TIME & WORK (4 Questions)

### Type 1: Simple Work Rate Problems

**Q6.** A can complete a job in 12 days, and B can complete the same job in 8 days. How long will it take if both work together?
A) 4.8 days
B) 5 days
C) 5.2 days
D) 6 days

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** A's rate = 1/12; B's rate = 1/8; Combined = 1/12 + 1/8 = 5/24; Time = 24/5 = 4.8 days

**Q7.** If 6 workers can build a wall in 20 days, how many days will it take 10 workers to build the same wall (assuming same efficiency)?
A) 10 days
B) 12 days
C) 15 days
D) 18 days

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Work = Workers × Days = 6 × 20 = 120 man-days; 10 workers need 120/10 = 12 days

### Type 2: Work with Efficiency Differences

**Q8.** A works twice as fast as B. If A and B together can complete a job in 8 days, how long will B take working alone?
A) 20 days
B) 24 days
C) 30 days
D) 36 days

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** If B's rate = 1/x, then A's rate = 2/x; Combined = 3/x = 1/8; x = 24 days

**Q9.** Pipes A and B fill a tank in 12 and 15 minutes respectively. Pipe C drains it in 20 minutes. If all three are opened simultaneously, in how much time will the tank be filled?
A) 8 minutes
B) 9 minutes
C) 10 minutes
D) 11 minutes

**Answer:** C | **Solution:** Net rate = 1/12 + 1/15 - 1/20 = (5 + 4 - 3)/60 = 6/60 = 1/10; Time = 10 minutes

## PART 3: PROFIT & LOSS (4 Questions)

### Type 1: Simple Profit/Loss Percentage

**Q10.** A shopkeeper buys goods for Rs. 400 and sells them for Rs. 560. What is his profit percentage?
A) 30%
B) 35%
C) 40%
D) 45%

**Answer:** C | **Solution:** Profit = 560 - 400 = 160; Profit% = (160/400) × 100 = 40%

**Q11.** If an item is sold at a loss of 15% for Rs. 340, what was the cost price?
A) Rs. 350
B) Rs. 380
C) Rs. 400
D) Rs. 420

**Answer:** C | **Solution:** SP = CP × (1 - 15/100); 340 = CP × 0.85; CP = 400

### Type 2: Markup, Discount & Successive Changes

**Q12.** A merchant marks his goods 25% above cost price and gives a discount of 10%. What is his profit percentage?
A) 12.5%
B) 13.75%
C) 15%
D) 17.5%

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Let CP = 100; MP = 125; SP = 125 × 0.9 = 112.5; Profit% = 12.5%

**Q13.** The price of an article increases by 20% and then decreases by 10%. What is the net percentage change?
A) 8% increase
B) 10% increase
C) 12% increase
D) 15% increase

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** Final = Initial × 1.2 × 0.9 = Initial × 1.08 = 8% increase

## PART 5: RATIO & PROPORTION (3 Questions)

**Q17.** Two numbers are in the ratio 5:8. If their difference is 36, find the larger number.
A) 90
B) 96
C) 100
D) 108

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Let numbers be 5x and 8x; 8x - 5x = 36; 3x = 36; x = 12; Larger = 8 × 12 = 96

**Q18.** A sum of Rs. 1200 is divided among A, B, and C in the ratio 2:3:5. How much does B receive?
A) Rs. 240
B) Rs. 360
C) Rs. 480
D) Rs. 600

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Total parts = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10; B's share = (3/10) × 1200 = 360

**Q19.** If x:y = 3:4 and y:z = 5:6, find x:y:z.
A) 15:20:24
B) 3:4:5
C) 5:6:7
D) 12:16:18

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** x:y = 3:4 (multiply by 5) = 15:20; y:z = 5:6 (multiply by 4) = 20:24; x:y:z = 15:20:24

## PART 6: SIMPLE & COMPOUND INTEREST (3 Questions)

**Q20.** What is the simple interest on Rs. 5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years?
A) Rs. 1000
B) Rs. 1200
C) Rs. 1400
D) Rs. 1600

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** SI = (P × R × T)/100 = (5000 × 8 × 3)/100 = 1200

**Q21.** A principal of Rs. 10,000 is invested at 10% compound interest per annum for 2 years. What is the amount?
A) Rs. 11,000
B) Rs. 11,100
C) Rs. 12,000
D) Rs. 12,100

**Answer:** D | **Solution:** A = P(1 + r/100)^t = 10000 × (1.1)^2 = 10000 × 1.21 = 12,100

**Q22.** At what rate of simple interest will Rs. 4000 become Rs. 4800 in 2 years?
A) 8% per annum
B) 10% per annum
C) 12% per annum
D) 15% per annum

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** SI = 4800 - 4000 = 800; Rate = (SI × 100)/(P × T) = (800 × 100)/(4000 × 2) = 10%

**Q23.** What is the next number in the series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?
A) 40
B) 42

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Differences are 4, 6, 8, 10, 12; Next = 30 + 12 = 42

**Q24.** If the sum of first n natural numbers is 55, what is n?
A) 10
B) 11

D) 13

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** n(n+1)/2 = 55; n(n+1) = 110; n = 10

## PART 8: MIXTURE & ALLEGATION (2 Questions)

**Q25.** 40 liters of a mixture contains milk and water in the ratio 3:2. How many liters of water should be added to make it 1:1?
A) 6 liters
B) 8 liters
C) 10 liters
D) 12 liters

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Milk = (3/5) × 40 = 24 liters; Water = 16 liters; Need 24 liters water; Add 24 - 16 = 8 liters

**Q26.** A merchant mixes two varieties of coffee costing Rs. 80/kg and Rs. 120/kg in the ratio 3:2. What is the cost price of the mixture?
A) Rs. 95/kg
B) Rs. 96/kg
C) Rs. 100/kg
D) Rs. 105/kg

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Cost = (3×80 + 2×120)/(3+2) = (240 + 240)/5 = 480/5 = 96

**Q27.** A die is rolled. What is the probability of getting a number greater than 4?
A) 1/2
B) 1/3
C) 2/3
D) 5/6

**Answer:** B | **Solution:** Favorable outcomes = 5, 6 (2 outcomes); Probability = 2/6 = 1/3

**Q28.** The area of a rectangle is 120 cm² and its length is 15 cm. What is its perimeter?
A) 46 cm
B) 50 cm
C) 52 cm
D) 54 cm

**Answer:** A | **Solution:** Width = 120/15 = 8 cm; Perimeter = 2(15 + 8) = 46 cm

## PREPARATION TIPS FOR NUMERICAL ABILITY:

1. **Memorize Key Formulas:** Distance = Speed × Time, SI = PRT/100, etc.
2. **Practice Speed Calculations:** Use mental math tricks, avoid calculator dependency
3. **Solve Type-wise Problems:** Master one type completely before moving to next
4. **Time Management:** Allocate 1.5-2 minutes per question (55 min for 28 Qs)
5. **Accuracy Over Speed:** 1 correct answer (3 marks) > 3 wrong answers (-3 marks)
6. **Common Mistakes:** Read problem carefully, check units, avoid arithmetic errors

**Total Practice Time:** 60 minutes for full mastery
