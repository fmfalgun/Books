## PART 2: ERROR DETECTION IN SENTENCES (4 Questions)

Identify the grammatically incorrect sentence.

**Q5.** 
A) The committee has decided to postpone the meeting until next week.
B) Neither of the two candidates are eligible for the position.
C) Each of the students were given a separate problem to solve.
D) The team have completed their assignments on time.

**Answer:** B & C (Singular verb required: "is eligible", "was given")

**Q6.**
A) The government is taking steps to reduce pollution in metropolitan cities.
B) The majority of people prefers coffee to tea in this region.
C) Everyone in the office have received their annual bonuses.
D) The data shows interesting patterns in consumer behavior.

**Answer:** B & C (Verb should be "prefer" and "has received")

**Q7.**
A) I would have gone to the party if I had been invited.
B) He insisted that he be allowed to participate in the debate.
C) The manager suggested that she goes on leave next month.
D) If you had told me earlier, I would have helped you.

**Answer:** C ("go" not "goes" in subjunctive mood)

**Q8.**
A) The book is more interesting than any other book I have read.
B) She is the most talented among all the students in the class.
C) His performance was worse than his brother.
D) The second option is better than the first option.

**Answer:** C (Should be "than his brother's" or "than that of his brother")

## PART 3: SENTENCE COMPLETION / FILLING IN CORRECT WORD (4 Questions)

**Q9.** The speaker's argument was so _______ that even his opponents could not help but acknowledge its validity.
A) nebulous
B) cogent
C) banal
D) turgid

**Answer:** B (cogent = persuasive, compelling)

**Q10.** Despite being a _______ individual, she demonstrated remarkable _______ when faced with adversity.
A) timid, cowardice
B) gregarious, introversion
C) reticent, fortitude
D) verbose, taciturnity

**Answer:** C (reticent = reserved; fortitude = courage/strength)

**Q11.** The government's _______ economic policies led to widespread _______ among the working class.
A) liberal, prosperity
B) stringent, discontent
C) benevolent, gratitude
D) progressive, stagnation

**Answer:** B (stringent = strict; discontent = dissatisfaction)

**Q12.** His _______ nature made him an excellent diplomat, as he could _______ even the most contentious disputes.
A) volatile, exacerbate
B) truculent, inflame
C) conciliatory, ameliorate
D) obstinate, resolve

**Answer:** C (conciliatory = peace-making; ameliorate = improve)

Reform movements eventually _____(Q18)_____ labor laws and social welfare systems, mitigating some of the worst excesses of early industrial capitalism.

D) neither

## PART 7: ANALOGY (2 Questions)

**Q21.** TEACHER : CLASSROOM :: ?
A) Doctor : Hospital
B) Chef : Kitchen
C) Lawyer : Court
D) All of the above

**Answer:** D (All are analogous relationships showing profession : workplace)

## PART 8: SENTENCE REARRANGEMENT / PARA JUMBLES (2 Questions)

**Q23.** Arrange the jumbled sentences in logical order:
A) The technology was initially met with skepticism from established players.
B) Blockchain emerged as a revolutionary distributed ledger technology.
C) Today, it powers cryptocurrencies, supply chain management, and smart contracts.
D) However, its transparency and security features gradually won over critics.

**Correct Order:** B → A → D → C

**Explanation:** Introduces blockchain (B) → Initial resistance (A) → Acceptance (D) → Current applications (C)

**Q24.** Arrange in proper sequence:
A) The Renaissance marked a revival of classical knowledge and artistic expression.
B) This intellectual movement later spread across Europe, transforming society.
C) It originated in Italy during the 14th century, driven by renewed interest in ancient Greek and Roman texts.
D) Artists, scholars, and philosophers challenged medieval worldviews and championed humanism.

**Correct Order:** A → C → D → B

**Explanation:** Definition (A) → Origin (C) → Key characteristics (D) → Geographic spread (B)

**PREPARATION TIPS FOR ENGLISH:**
1. Build vocabulary: Learn 20-30 new words weekly with context
2. Read comprehensions actively: Take 2-3 min per passage, then answer questions
3. Practice error detection daily: Focus on subject-verb agreement and tenses
4. Master idioms: Create flashcards of 50+ common English idioms
5. Time management: Allocate 50 minutes for 25-30 questions (1.5-2 min per question)

**Total Practice Time:** 50-60 minutes for full mastery
